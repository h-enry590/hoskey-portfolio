My Philosophy

Stories That Stick. Vision That Moves.

I believe every story deserves to be seen, heard, and felt. My journey began in the quiet village of Walewale in northern Ghana. A place where dreams are big and resources are few. But I knew one thing: I was born to create.

From those early days to now, I have carried one belief: that even the smallest beginnings can create a lasting impact. Every frame I shoot, every sound I engineer, every story I tell is driven by honesty, originality, and the power of human connection.

Motto: "Capturing Moments, Sharing Impact"

Life did not start easy for me. Growing up in Walewale, Northern Ghana, I knew what it meant to want. To dream big when the world around you offers little. There were days when the road ahead felt impossible, when the weight of circumstance pressed heavy on my shoulders. But even in those moments, I held onto something I could not quite name: a fire that refused to go out.

I remember my first encounter with a camera. It was not mine. It was not even meant for me. But the moment I looked through that viewfinder, I saw the world differently. I saw that stories could be captured, moments could be frozen, and impact could be shared. That moment changed everything.

I taught myself. I stumbled. I fell. I got back up. Every mistake became a lesson, every failure a stepping stone. I worked small gigs, took on every opportunity that came my way, and slowly: painfully slowly at times: I began to build something. From a borrowed camera to my own equipment. From village gatherings to international events. From a boy with a dream to a man with a company.

Today, I stand here not because the road was easy, but because I refused to give up. Hoskey Production is more than a business. It is proof that where you start does not determine where you end. It is a message to every young person in Walewale, in Ghana, in Africa: your circumstances do not define your destiny. Your determination does.

I am still climbing. Still learning. Still growing. But I carry with me the lessons of every struggle, the gratitude for every helping hand, and the vision of a future where stories from every corner of this continent can reach the world.

Roots & Heritage

The Abu Family
of Walewale

A legacy of leadership, honor, and community spanning generations.

Where It All Began

The Abu family calls Walewale home. Located in the Nayiri Fong area near the Walewale main market, this family has stood as a pillar of the community for generations.

The Abu family is one of the most respected families in Walewale's history, with over five different chiefs in Walewale's traditional lineage. Their influence and leadership have shaped the community for decades, earning them a place of honor in the region.

5+

1
Most Respected Family
"The Abu family has long been a symbol of dignity and service in Walewale. Their name carries weight, their word carries honor, and their legacy carries hope."

Abu Yahaya is the father of Ziblim Abu James. The values passed down through the Abu lineage: integrity, perseverance, and community service: are the same values that drive Hoskey Production today.

From the chiefs who led the people to the sons who carry the name across borders, the Abu family story is one of resilience, pride, and progress. It is the foundation upon which Ziblim built his dreams.
